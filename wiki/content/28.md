# Using a Mask

With a Mask, you can clean what you don't like or draw only want you want.

Example with this Front View:
![{BD22AC63-0597-4230-89AC-6B50B55A748E}](http://localhost:3001/wiki-media/1781200131370-518770217.png)

Click on "Draw Mask" button
![{EACCA872-DE56-4673-B94D-DB3AB295AF4B}](http://localhost:3001/wiki-media/1781200335983-154123871.png)

Look below the face
![{148AD0E3-502A-4E97-AAAF-34FE789ABAEF}](http://localhost:3001/wiki-media/1781200274751-724303498.png)

If you just want to clean some parts, click on the button "Fill Mesh" (It will will create a mask that fills the whole mesh).
![{CF322096-ACB1-47AD-99D1-F6B6FDCF5E77}](http://localhost:3001/wiki-media/1781200373891-208755454.png)

Then click on "Erase" button.
Erase what you don't like.

![{1CE1E845-09BE-4F7B-BA1C-9A1A735BFFD1}](http://localhost:3001/wiki-media/1781200433187-501241991.png)

When you have finished, click on "Exit" button or on "Drawing button"
